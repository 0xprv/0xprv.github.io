# Using A.I Computer Vision (ViT) Transformer in Detecting 100s of Plant Diseases

**Written on:** 15 Jan 2024  

---

Now, it's heartwarming to acknowledge how swiftly AI is advancing. However, in the realm of agriculture, the solutions are still a bit scarce. This scarcity underlines the current challenge we face in addressing agricultural issues. Consequently, our farmers continue to grapple with familiar problems, reminiscent of those they encountered a decade or even two decades ago.

Therefore, with a deep sense of purpose, we embarked on a journey to alleviate challenges in the agricultural domain. Initially, our efforts led us to develop AI systems tailored for drones and edge devices. However, our commitment to making a positive impact has grown even stronger. We have envisioned a network of interconnected apps and software that will directly address the pressing issues that farmers presently encounter. Through meaningful interactions with farmers, we aim to foster a collaborative approach.

Furthermore, our strategy includes making these innovative AI solutions widely accessible. We have devised a plan to offer both free and affordable versions of these AI-powered applications. This approach ensures that anyone with a mobile device can effortlessly download and benefit from these applications, irrespective of their technological background.

You might be curious about the origins of the data we utilize and the range of diseases our AI is currently capable of predicting. Our journey involved training on an extensive dataset of approximately **200,000 images**. This meticulous process has enabled our AI to accurately identify a diverse array of plant diseases.

## Data Collection

This dataset is gathered from:
- Open internet datasets  
- Direct farmer contributions  
- Field images with disease annotations  

Each sample includes:
- Plant name  
- Disease label  
- Healthy vs infected classification
